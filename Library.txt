 library management system (LMS) should be able to:
Store and retrieve information: Store and retrieve information about library patrons and books 
Search for books: Search for books by title, author, or ISBN 
Check out and return books: Allow users to check out and return books 
Display book lists: Display a list of all books in the library 
Integrate with other systems: Integrate with other systems to reduce duplicate entries and streamline operations 
Scale and adapt: Scale and adapt to accommodate growth in the collection, number of patrons, and changes in services 
Be user-friendly: Be easy to use and have a graphic user interface 
Maintain data: Maintain data accurately and easily accessible 
Save time: Save time by automating tasks like finding books and issuing books
